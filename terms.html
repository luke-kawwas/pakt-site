Program Description

Pakt's accountability partner feature allows users to designate one or more phone numbers to receive SMS text message alerts when a pakt is broken. These messages are sent on behalf of the Pakt user who created the pakt and added the recipient's number.

SMS messages are sent by Pakt and are triggered solely by pakt activity — specifically, when a user opens a restricted app during an active pakt period.

Message Frequency

Accountability partners may receive up to 3 SMS messages per day. Message volume varies based on pakt activity. Messages are only sent when a pakt is broken — no promotional or marketing messages are sent.

Consent

The Pakt app user who adds an accountability partner's phone number confirms, at the time of entry, that:

  • They have obtained the explicit prior consent of the recipient to receive SMS messages from Pakt.
  • The recipient is aware of the nature and frequency of the messages they may receive.
  • They are authorized to provide the recipient's phone number for this purpose.

Pakt does not independently verify consent with the recipient. Full responsibility for obtaining consent rests with the Pakt user adding the phone number.

How to Opt Out

Recipients can stop receiving SMS messages at any time using either of the following methods:

  • Reply STOP to any SMS message received from Pakt. No further messages will be sent to that number.
  • Ask the Pakt user who added their number to remove it from the app.

After opting out via STOP, if you wish to re-enable messages, reply START to the same number.

For help, reply HELP to any message or contact us at support@pakt.app.
